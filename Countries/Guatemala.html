Guatemala faces a range of environmental challenges, including deforestation, soil degradation, and water pollution. Widespread logging, agricultural expansion, and urbanization contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Soil degradation, intensified by unsustainable farming practices, poses risks to agricultural productivity. Water pollution results from industrial discharges, agricultural runoff, and inadequate waste management, impacting water quality. Guatemala is also vulnerable to natural disasters, such as hurricanes and earthquakes, which further exacerbate environmental issues. Sustainable land management, reforestation initiatives, improved agricultural practices, and better waste management are essential for addressing these eco problems in Guatemala.
