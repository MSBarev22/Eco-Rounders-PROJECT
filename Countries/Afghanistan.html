Afghanistan faces significant ecological challenges including deforestation, water scarcity, soil degradation, loss of biodiversity, pollution, and susceptibility to natural disasters like earthquakes and droughts. These issues are exacerbated by factors such as unsustainable practices, conflict, inadequate infrastructure, and climate change. Addressing these challenges requires sustainable land management, conservation efforts, improved water management, and mitigation of environmental degradation. However, the ongoing conflict and socio-economic issues make these solutions complex to implement.
Afghanistan is a landlocked country located in South Asia and Central Asia. It is bordered by Pakistan to the east and south, Iran to the west, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, and Tajikistan to the north, and China to the northeast. The country has a diverse landscape, including mountains, plains, and deserts.
