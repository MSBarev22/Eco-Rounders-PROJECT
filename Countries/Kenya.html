Top Ecological Countries
Kenya faces environmental challenges, including deforestation, soil erosion, and wildlife conservation issues. Widespread logging and agricultural activities contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Soil erosion, intensified by unsustainable farming practices, impacts agricultural productivity. Wildlife conservation is a concern due to poaching, habitat loss, and human-wildlife conflicts. Water pollution from industrial discharges and inadequate waste management is also a pressing issue. Kenya is actively addressing these eco problems through reforestation efforts, sustainable agricultural practices, wildlife conservation initiatives, and measures to improve water quality.
