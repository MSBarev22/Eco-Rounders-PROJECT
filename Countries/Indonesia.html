Indonesia grapples with significant environmental challenges, including deforestation, air and water pollution, and habitat loss. Rampant logging and palm oil plantations contribute to widespread deforestation, impacting biodiversity and indigenous communities. Air pollution, especially in urban areas, is driven by industrial emissions and transportation. Water pollution results from agricultural runoff and improper waste disposal. Indonesia's diverse ecosystems, including coral reefs and rainforests, face threats from unsustainable practices. Efforts to address these eco problems involve sustainable forestry practices, stricter environmental regulations, and conservation initiatives to protect the country's rich natural heritage.
