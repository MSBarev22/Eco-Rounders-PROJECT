Ireland faces environmental challenges, including water pollution, habitat loss, and climate change impacts. Agriculture contributes to water pollution through nutrient runoff, impacting rivers and lakes. Urbanization and infrastructure development lead to habitat loss for wildlife. Climate change poses risks such as extreme weather events and sea-level rise. Ireland is working on sustainable agriculture practices, conservation initiatives, and efforts to reduce greenhouse gas emissions to address these eco problems and enhance environmental resilience.
