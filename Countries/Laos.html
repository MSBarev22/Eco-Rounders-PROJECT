Laos faces environmental challenges, including deforestation, habitat loss, and water pollution. Rampant logging and agricultural expansion contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Habitat loss, driven by infrastructure development and mining, further impacts wildlife. Water pollution results from industrial discharges and agricultural runoff. Laos is actively working on sustainable forestry practices, conservation efforts, and improved water management to address these eco problems and enhance environmental sustainability. Additionally, the country is vulnerable to natural disasters, adding another layer of complexity to its environmental concerns.
