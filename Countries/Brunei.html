Brunei faces multifaceted economic challenges, primarily anchored in its heavy reliance on oil exports, rendering the economy vulnerable to global energy price volatility. The country's limited economic diversification, coupled with overdependence on oil revenues, heightens the risk of economic instability and impedes sustainable growth prospects. While boasting a relatively low unemployment rate, there exists a pressing need to bridge the gap between available skills and those demanded by a diversified economy, aiming to harness the potential of a skilled workforce. Additionally, addressing youth employment remains pivotal for long-term economic sustainability and fostering inclusive growth. Effectively tackling these challenges requires comprehensive strategies focusing on economic diversification, skill development, and youth empowerment to ensure a more resilient and sustainable economic future for Brunei.
Brunei is a small sovereign state located on the island of Borneo in Southeast Asia. Bandar Seri Begawan is the capital and largest city. The population is approximately 430,000 people, primarily speaking Malay. Brunei operates as a constitutional sultanate, with the Sultan as the head of state and government.
