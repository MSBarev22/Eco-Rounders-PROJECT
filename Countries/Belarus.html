Belarus confronts several economic challenges, notably its heavy reliance on trade with Russia, which exposes it to fluctuations in the Russian economy. The dominance of state-controlled enterprises and centralized planning impedes competitiveness, innovation, and efficiency within its economy. Moreover, structural limitations pose obstacles to economic diversification and sustainable growth, necessitating reforms to address these underlying issues.
Belarus is a landlocked country in Eastern Europe, bordered by Russia to the northeast, Ukraine to the south, Poland to the west, Lithuania and Latvia to the northwest, and shares a small border with Estonia. Minsk is the capital and largest city. The population is around 9.3 million people, primarily speaking Belarusian and Russian. Belarus operates as a presidential republic. The economy relies significantly on manufacturing, particularly in machinery, chemicals, and agriculture, and it has historical and cultural ties with its neighboring countries.
