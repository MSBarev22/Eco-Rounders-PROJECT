Fiji faces environmental challenges, including coral reef degradation, deforestation, and vulnerability to climate change. Coral reefs, crucial for marine biodiversity, suffer from overfishing, pollution, and rising sea temperatures. Deforestation, driven by agriculture and logging, threatens terrestrial ecosystems and contributes to soil erosion. Climate change intensifies risks, with Fiji experiencing more frequent cyclones and rising sea levels, posing threats to coastal communities. Addressing these eco problems requires concerted efforts in sustainable fishing, reforestation, and climate resilience strategies to safeguard Fiji's unique ecosystems and communities.
