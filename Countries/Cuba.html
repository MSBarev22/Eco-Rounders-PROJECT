Cuba grapples with a range of environmental challenges that affect its unique ecosystems. Deforestation, largely driven by agriculture, logging, and urban expansion, poses a threat to the country's diverse flora and fauna. Soil degradation is exacerbated by unsustainable agricultural practices, impacting the fertility and resilience of the land. Coastal areas face challenges from habitat loss and pollution, intensified by urban development and industrial activities.
Cuba faces environmental challenges, including deforestation from agriculture and urban expansion, soil degradation due to unsustainable farming practices, and coastal habitat loss and pollution.
