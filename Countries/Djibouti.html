Djibouti, situated at the crossroads of Africa and the Middle East, grapples with a range of environmental challenges. Water scarcity is a critical issue, exacerbated by a semi-arid climate, limited freshwater resources, and high evaporation rates. The country's dependence on desalination and vulnerable groundwater sources heightens concerns about sustainable water management. Land degradation and desertification pose significant threats, driven by overgrazing, deforestation, and agricultural practices that strain the fragile ecosystems. This not only impacts biodiversity but also reduces the productivity of arable land. The fragile coastal and marine environments face pressures from overfishing, habitat degradation, and pollution, including plastic waste.
