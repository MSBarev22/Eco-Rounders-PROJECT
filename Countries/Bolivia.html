Bolivia faces economic challenges stemming from heavy dependency on commodities like natural gas and minerals, persistent poverty and inequality, inadequate infrastructure, periodic political instability affecting policies and investments, and the issue of coca production and drug trafficking, impacting social and economic development within the country.
Bolivia is a South American country, bordered by Brazil, Paraguay, Argentina, Chile, and Peru. Its administrative capital is La Paz, while Sucre serves as the constitutional capital. With a population of around 11 million people speaking Spanish and indigenous languages, Bolivia operates as a presidential republic.
