Liberia confronts environmental challenges, including deforestation, soil degradation, and water pollution. Rampant logging and agricultural expansion contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Unsustainable farming practices intensify soil degradation, impacting agricultural productivity. Water pollution results from industrial discharges and inadequate waste management, affecting water quality. Liberia is also susceptible to deforestation-driven loss of biodiversity and climate change impacts. Sustainable land management, reforestation initiatives, improved agricultural practices, and stricter environmental regulations are essential for addressing these eco problems and enhancing environmental sustainability in Liberia.
