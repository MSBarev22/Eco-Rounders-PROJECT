Ghana confronts environmental challenges, including deforestation, soil degradation, and water pollution. Rampant logging and agricultural expansion contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Soil degradation, aggravated by unsustainable farming practices, poses a threat to agricultural productivity. Water pollution results from industrial discharges and inadequate waste management, impacting water quality. Additionally, illegal mining activities, particularly in gold mining, contribute to environmental degradation. Sustainable land management, reforestation initiatives, improved agricultural practices, and stricter environmental regulations are essential for addressing these eco problems in Ghana.
