Top Ecological Countries
Guinea faces environmental challenges such as deforestation, soil erosion, and water pollution. Logging and agricultural activities contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Unsustainable farming practices exacerbate soil erosion, impacting agricultural productivity. Water pollution results from inadequate waste management and industrial discharges, affecting water quality. Guinea's vulnerability to climate change and susceptibility to natural disasters underscore the importance of sustainable land management, reforestation efforts, improved waste practices, and conservation measures to address these eco problems and enhance environmental sustainability.
