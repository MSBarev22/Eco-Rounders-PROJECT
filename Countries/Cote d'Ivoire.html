Top Ecological Countries
Côte d'Ivoire faces a range of environmental challenges that impact its diverse ecosystems. Deforestation is a significant issue, driven by agricultural expansion, logging, and cocoa cultivation. The latter, a major economic driver, has contributed to the loss of forests, biodiversity, and soil degradation. Illegal logging further exacerbates deforestation, posing threats to the country's rich flora and fauna. Water pollution is a pressing concern, primarily due to industrial discharges and inadequate waste management, affecting both freshwater sources and coastal areas. Côte d'Ivoire's delicate environmental balance is compounded by issues such as soil erosion, habitat degradation, and the impacts of climate change. Addressing these challenges requires a comprehensive approach that balances economic development with sustainable environmental practices and conservation efforts.
