Comoros, a picturesque archipelago in the Indian Ocean, grapples with a series of environmental challenges that shape the delicate balance of its ecosystems. Deforestation stands as a prominent concern, fueled by the expansion of agricultural activities, logging practices, and increasing human settlements. This widespread clearance of forests not only jeopardizes biodiversity but also accelerates soil erosion, leading to the degradation of arable land and exacerbating the vulnerability of the islands to natural disasters.
Comoros faces environmental challenges such as deforestation driven by agriculture and logging, resulting in habitat loss and soil erosion. Limited water resources, exacerbated by population growth and inefficient management, contribute to periodic water scarcity. The island nation grapples with a delicate balance between preserving biodiversity and sustaining its agricultural practices.
