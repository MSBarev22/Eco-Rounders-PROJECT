Bangladesh encounters economic challenges including persistent poverty, income inequality, high unemployment especially among youth, deficient infrastructure, environmental vulnerability to natural disasters, widespread corruption, and garment industry issues like worker safety and global competition, all impacting the country's economic growth and stability.
Bangladesh is a country located in South Asia, bordered by India to the west, north, and east, Myanmar to the southeast, and the Bay of Bengal to the south. Dhaka is the capital and largest city. The population is approximately 170 million people, primarily speaking Bengali. Bangladesh operates as a parliamentary democracy. The economy relies on sectors such as agriculture, textiles, manufacturing, and increasingly, the service industry. Bangladesh is known for its rich cultural heritage and as a major exporter of textiles and garments.
