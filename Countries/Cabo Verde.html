Cabo Verde grapples with an array of economic challenges impacting its developmental trajectory. The nation's heavy reliance on tourism, while a vital economic driver, poses vulnerability to external shocks. This dependence makes the economy susceptible to global economic fluctuations and natural disasters, potentially destabilizing overall economic stability and growth prospects. As a small island state, Cabo Verde faces heightened susceptibility to climate change, adversely affecting agricultural productivity and exacerbating food insecurity due to constraints in arable land and water resources. Consequently, there is an increased dependency on food imports for sustenance, impacting the country's self-sufficiency.
Cabo Verde, an island nation in the Atlantic Ocean, relies on tourism and faces challenges due to its dependence on this industry, vulnerability to external factors, limited natural resources impacting agriculture, high unemployment rates, especially among the youth, and issues in maintaining debt sustainability, influencing its economic stability and growth.
