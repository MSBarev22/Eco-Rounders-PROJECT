Australia faces various economic challenges, including fluctuating commodity prices affecting its resource-dependent economy, income inequality, housing affordability issues, environmental concerns like droughts and wildfires impacting agriculture, dependence on China as a major trading partner, and the need for sustainable policies to address climate change while balancing economic growth.
Australia, located in the Southern Hemisphere, is the world's sixth-largest country by land area. The capital is Canberra, while major cities include Sydney, Melbourne, Brisbane, and Perth. The population is approximately 25 million people, predominantly speaking English. It operates as a federal parliamentary democracy and constitutional monarchy, with Queen Elizabeth II as the ceremonial head of state. Australia's economy is diverse, relying on sectors like mining, agriculture, services, and technology, while also being known for its rich biodiversity and natural landscapes.
