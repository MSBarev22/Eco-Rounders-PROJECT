The broader economic challenges in the Bahamas encompass heavy dependence on tourism, susceptibility to climate change effects such as rising sea levels and natural disasters, income inequality, high unemployment rates, and the need for economic diversification to ensure sustainable growth and resilience.
The Bahamas is an archipelago located in the Atlantic Ocean, comprising 700 islands and cays. Nassau is the capital and largest city. The population is approximately 400,000 people, primarily speaking English. The Bahamas operates as a parliamentary democracy and constitutional monarchy, with a tourism-dependent economy, along with sectors like financial services and fishing. Known for its beautiful beaches and tourism attractions, the Bahamas is a popular destination for travelers.
