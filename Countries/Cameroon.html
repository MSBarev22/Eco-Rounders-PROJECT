Cameroon faces a multitude of economic challenges hindering its development. The country's heavy dependence on oil exports and agriculture makes it susceptible to global price fluctuations and climate changes, impacting revenue stability and food security. Insufficient infrastructure, particularly in transportation, energy, and telecommunications, hampers economic growth and development efforts. High unemployment rates, especially among the youth, contribute significantly to widespread poverty, affecting socio-economic stability. Moreover, periodic political tensions and internal conflicts disrupt economic activities, posing barriers to attracting foreign investments crucial for sustained growth. Addressing these multifaceted challenges necessitates comprehensive strategies focusing on economic diversification, infrastructure development, job creation, poverty alleviation, and political stability to foster sustainable economic growth and prosperity in Cameroon.
