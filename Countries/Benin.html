Certainly, Benin encounters multifaceted economic challenges, including pervasive high levels of poverty and unemployment, particularly among the youth, while grappling with a significant dependence on agriculture as a primary economic sector. This agricultural reliance exposes the nation to vulnerabilities stemming from climate change impacts, which, in turn, jeopardize food security and livelihoods, collectively posing substantial barriers to achieving sustainable development and enduring economic stability within the country.
West African country, borders Togo, Nigeria, Niger, Burkina Faso, and Gulf of Guinea. Capital: Porto-Novo, Largest City: Cotonou. Population: ~12 million, French-speaking. Economy: agriculture, trade, services. Rich cultural heritage from Dahomey Kingdom.
