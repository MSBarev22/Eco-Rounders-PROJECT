Jordan faces environmental challenges such as water scarcity, soil erosion, and desertification. With limited water resources and a growing population, water scarcity is a significant issue, affecting agriculture and communities. Unsustainable agricultural practices contribute to soil erosion and desertification, impacting arable land. Urbanization and industrial activities also pose challenges to air and water quality. Jordan's vulnerability to climate change further exacerbates these eco problems. The country is working on water conservation measures, sustainable agriculture practices, and environmental initiatives to address these challenges and enhance environmental sustainability.
