Top Ecological Countries
Finland contends with several environmental challenges, including air and water pollution, habitat loss, and climate change impacts. Despite its commitment to sustainability, industrial activities contribute to air pollution, affecting air quality and posing health risks. Water pollution is an issue due to agricultural runoff and discharges from pulp and paper mills. Habitat loss, driven by urbanization and forestry, threatens biodiversity, particularly in sensitive ecosystems like peatlands and old-growth forests. Climate change exacerbates these problems, leading to shifts in ecosystems, more frequent extreme weather events, and challenges for traditional livelihoods like reindeer herding. To address these eco problems, Finland is working on stricter environmental regulations, promoting sustainable forestry practices, and investing in renewable energy to mitigate its ecological impact and enhance overall environmental sustainability.
