The Gambia faces environmental challenges such as deforestation, soil erosion, and water scarcity. Deforestation, driven by logging and agriculture, contributes to habitat loss and biodiversity decline. Soil erosion poses threats to agricultural productivity, affecting food security. Water scarcity is exacerbated by climate change and increased demand, impacting both rural and urban communities. Addressing these eco problems in The Gambia requires sustainable land management practices, reforestation initiatives, and efficient water resource management to promote environmental resilience and safeguard the well-being of its population.
