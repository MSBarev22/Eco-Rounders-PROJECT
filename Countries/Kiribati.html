Kiribati faces pressing environmental challenges, primarily due to climate change. As a low-lying island nation in the Pacific, Kiribati is highly vulnerable to rising sea levels, leading to saltwater intrusion, coastal erosion, and the loss of arable land. Limited freshwater resources and inadequate waste management also contribute to environmental stress. These issues threaten the livelihoods and well-being of Kiribati's population. The country is actively involved in international climate change initiatives, emphasizing adaptation strategies, sustainable water management, and efforts to mitigate the impact of environmental challenges on its people and ecosystems.
