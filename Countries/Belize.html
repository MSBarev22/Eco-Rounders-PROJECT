Belize encounters various economic challenges including significant public debt, susceptibility to natural disasters affecting critical industries like tourism and agriculture, limited economic diversification, high youth unemployment rates, and the necessity for sustainable development strategies to mitigate environmental issues and promote enduring economic growth and stability. Addressing these challenges requires comprehensive approaches focusing on diversified economic strategies and environmental sustainability.
Belize, situated in Central America, has Belmopan as its capital. The population is about 400,000, mainly speaking English. It operates as a parliamentary democracy with an economy driven by agriculture, tourism, and services, known for its cultural diversity and natural attractions like the Belize Barrier Reef.
