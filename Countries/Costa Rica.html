Costa Rica, renowned for its rich biodiversity and commitment to conservation, faces challenges that include deforestation, largely driven by agricultural expansion. The conversion of forests into farmland poses a threat to diverse ecosystems and the species they harbor. Despite progressive policies promoting sustainable practices, illegal logging persists, contributing to habitat loss. Water pollution also arises from agricultural runoff and inadequate wastewater management, impacting both terrestrial and marine environments. The country grapples with striking a balance between agricultural development and environmental preservation, highlighting the ongoing efforts required to sustain its impressive biodiversity and ecological health.
