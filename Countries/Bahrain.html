Bahrain's broader economic challenges involve heavy reliance on oil revenues, urgency for economic diversification, high youth unemployment, income disparity, regional geopolitical tensions affecting stability, and the necessity for reforms to foster a more diverse and resilient economy.
Bahrain is a small island country located in the Persian Gulf, comprising multiple islands, with Manama as its capital and largest city. The population is approximately 1.7 million people, predominantly speaking Arabic. Bahrain operates as a constitutional monarchy. The economy is diversified, relying on industries such as oil and gas, financial services, tourism, and manufacturing. Known for its pearls and rich cultural heritage, Bahrain serves as a regional financial hub.
