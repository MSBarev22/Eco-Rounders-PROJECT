Jamaica grapples with environmental challenges, including deforestation, coral reef degradation, and water pollution. Unsustainable logging practices contribute to deforestation, impacting ecosystems and biodiversity. Coral reefs face threats from overfishing, pollution, and climate change, affecting marine ecosystems. Water pollution, particularly from agricultural runoff and inadequate waste management, poses risks to rivers and coastal areas. Jamaica's vulnerability to hurricanes adds to these environmental concerns. Efforts to address these eco problems involve sustainable forestry practices, coral reef conservation, and improved waste management to enhance environmental sustainability in Jamaica.
