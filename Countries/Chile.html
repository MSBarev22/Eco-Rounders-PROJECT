Chile faces a complex intersection of environmental and economic challenges. Persistent water scarcity, exacerbated by climate change and over-extraction for agriculture, poses a significant threat to both ecosystems and the livelihoods of communities, particularly in the northern regions. Deforestation, driven by logging and urbanization, contributes to biodiversity loss and soil erosion. Urban areas, notably Santiago, grapple with high levels of air pollution, primarily stemming from vehicle emissions and industrial activities. As a major global copper producer, Chile's mining sector contributes to environmental concerns, including habitat disruption and water pollution. These environmental issues are often intertwined with social challenges, such as economic inequality.
