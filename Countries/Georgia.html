Georgia faces diverse environmental challenges, including deforestation, soil erosion, and pollution. Deforestation, driven by logging and agricultural expansion, poses a threat to biodiversity and disrupts ecosystems. Soil erosion, exacerbated by unsustainable land-use practices, impacts agricultural productivity and leads to sedimentation in water bodies. Pollution, particularly in urban areas, affects air and water quality, posing risks to both the environment and public health. Climate change further intensifies these challenges, with increased frequency of extreme weather events. Addressing these eco problems in Georgia requires sustainable land management, reforestation efforts, pollution control measures, and climate resilience strategies.
