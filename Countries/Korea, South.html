South Korea grapples with environmental challenges, including air pollution, water contamination, and biodiversity loss. Urban areas, particularly Seoul, experience air pollution from industrial emissions and transportation. Water pollution results from industrial discharges and agricultural runoff. Rapid urbanization and infrastructure development contribute to habitat loss and fragmentation, impacting biodiversity. South Korea is actively addressing these eco problems through air quality improvement initiatives, water management measures, and conservation efforts to protect natural habitats and enhance environmental sustainability.
