Japan encounters environmental challenges, including air pollution, water pollution, and biodiversity loss. Urban areas, particularly Tokyo, experience air pollution from industrial activities and transportation. Water pollution results from industrial discharges and agricultural runoff. Biodiversity faces threats from habitat destruction and invasive species. Japan is susceptible to natural disasters, such as earthquakes and tsunamis, further impacting the environment. Efforts to address these eco problems include strict environmental regulations, sustainable practices, and initiatives for biodiversity conservation to enhance Japan's environmental sustainability.
