Senegal faces ecological challenges, including deforestation, soil erosion, and threats to biodiversity. Addressing these issues involves sustainable land management practices, afforestation initiatives, and biodiversity conservation efforts. Strengthening waste management, promoting renewable energy sources, and engaging communities in environmental stewardship contribute to resilience. Public awareness campaigns and educational initiatives play a key role in fostering sustainable practices. Collaboration with international partners and neighboring countries is crucial for Senegal to navigate its ecological challenges, ensuring the preservation of its diverse ecosystems and building a more sustainable and balanced future for this West African nation.
