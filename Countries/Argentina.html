Argentina's larger economic problems also encompass issues like currency depreciation, political instability leading to uncertainty in economic policies, challenges in debt management, corruption affecting various sectors, inadequate infrastructure, environmental concerns, and social inequalities, all of which contribute to the nation's complex economic landscape.
Argentina, located in South America, boasts Buenos Aires as its capital. With a population of around 45 million, Spanish serves as the official language. It operates as a federal presidential republic, featuring a diverse economy reliant on agriculture and various industries.
