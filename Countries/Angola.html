Angola encounters significant ecological issues, notably deforestation stemming from agricultural expansion, logging, and rapid urban growth. These activities result in substantial biodiversity loss, soil erosion, and degradation of habitats, posing severe environmental challenges in the country. Additionally, inadequate waste management and pollution further contribute to Angola's ecological concerns, necessitating sustainable strategies for environmental conservation and management.
Angola, located in Southern Africa with Luanda as its capital, has an estimated population of around 32-33 million people. Portuguese serves as the official language, and the country operates under a republic form of government.
