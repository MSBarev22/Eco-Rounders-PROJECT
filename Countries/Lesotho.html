Lesotho encounters environmental challenges, including soil erosion, deforestation, and water scarcity. Unsustainable agricultural practices contribute to soil erosion, impacting agricultural productivity. Deforestation, driven by logging and land-use changes, poses threats to biodiversity and ecosystems. Water scarcity is a significant concern due to factors such as climate change, population growth, and over-extraction. Lesotho is actively working on sustainable land management practices, reforestation efforts, and water conservation initiatives to address these eco problems and enhance environmental sustainability. Additionally, the country faces challenges related to climate change impacts and vulnerability to natural disasters.
