North Korea confronts various environmental challenges, including deforestation, soil erosion, and water pollution. Widespread logging and agricultural practices contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Unsustainable farming methods intensify soil erosion, impacting agricultural productivity. Water pollution results from industrial discharges and inadequate waste management. North Korea's closed political system limits access to information on specific environmental issues, but reports suggest challenges related to resource mismanagement and degradation. Addressing these eco problems requires sustainable land and water management, reforestation efforts, and improved waste practices.
