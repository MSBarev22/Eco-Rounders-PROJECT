Grenada faces environmental challenges, including coastal erosion, deforestation, and coral reef degradation. Rising sea levels and extreme weather events contribute to coastal erosion, impacting communities and infrastructure. Deforestation, driven by agriculture and urbanization, threatens biodiversity and disrupts ecosystems. Coral reef degradation, caused by factors like overfishing and climate change, poses risks to marine ecosystems. Sustainable land management, conservation efforts, and climate resilience strategies are crucial for addressing these eco problems in Grenada and safeguarding its natural resources.
