Guyana faces environmental challenges, including deforestation, habitat loss, and pollution. Logging, agriculture, and mining contribute to deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Habitat loss is a concern due to urbanization and agricultural expansion. Pollution, particularly in water bodies, results from mining activities and inadequate waste management. Guyana's vulnerability to climate change, including rising sea levels and extreme weather events, adds to these challenges. Sustainable land management, conservation efforts, improved waste practices, and climate resilience strategies are essential for addressing these eco problems and fostering environmental sustainability in Guyana.
