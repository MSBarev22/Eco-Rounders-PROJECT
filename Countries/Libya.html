Libya faces environmental challenges, including desertification, water scarcity, and oil-related pollution. Desertification, exacerbated by overgrazing and agricultural practices, threatens arable land. Water scarcity is a significant concern due to limited freshwater resources and inefficient water management. Oil-related pollution, resulting from the extraction and processing of petroleum, poses risks to the environment. Libya's political instability and conflicts further complicate environmental management efforts. Sustainable land management, water conservation measures, and pollution control initiatives are essential for addressing these eco problems and enhancing environmental sustainability in Libya.
