Barbados grapples with economic challenges such as high public debt levels, heavily relying on tourism which leaves it vulnerable to global economic fluctuations, susceptibility to external economic shocks, high unemployment rates, and the pressing need for economic diversification to enhance resilience and reduce dependency on a single industry for sustained growth.
Barbados is an island country situated in the Caribbean region of North America. Bridgetown serves as its capital and largest city. The population is approximately 290,000 people, primarily speaking English. Barbados operates as a parliamentary democracy and constitutional monarchy, with a diversified economy centered around tourism, agriculture (sugar production), manufacturing, and financial services. Known for its beautiful beaches and vibrant culture, Barbados is a popular tourist destination.
