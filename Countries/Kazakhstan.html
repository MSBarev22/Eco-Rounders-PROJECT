Kazakhstan faces environmental challenges, including land degradation, water scarcity, and air pollution. Unsustainable agricultural practices contribute to land degradation, impacting soil quality and biodiversity. Water scarcity is a concern, exacerbated by over-extraction and inefficient water management. Industrial activities and urbanization contribute to air pollution, affecting both urban and rural areas. Kazakhstan is working on sustainable land management practices, water conservation initiatives, and stricter environmental regulations to address these eco problems and enhance environmental sustainability.
