Israel grapples with environmental challenges that include water scarcity, desertification, and pollution. The arid climate and growing population contribute to water scarcity, leading to innovative water management solutions. Desertification poses a threat, with overgrazing and agricultural practices impacting arable land. Urban and industrial activities contribute to pollution, affecting air and water quality. Israel has implemented measures such as water recycling and afforestation to address these challenges and enhance environmental sustainability. Additionally, the geopolitical context in the region introduces complexities related to resource management and conservation.
