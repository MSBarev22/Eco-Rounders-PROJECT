Azerbaijan's larger economic challenges encompass heavy reliance on oil and gas exports, high unemployment rates, the imperative need for economic diversification, regional conflicts affecting stability, and widespread corruption hindering sectoral development and overall economic progress.
Azerbaijan is situated in the South Caucasus region of Eurasia, bordered by Russia, Georgia, Armenia, Iran, and the Caspian Sea. Baku serves as its capital and largest city. The population is approximately 10 million, with Azerbaijani being the primary language spoken. Azerbaijan operates as a unitary semi-presidential republic. The economy relies significantly on oil and gas exports, along with sectors such as agriculture and manufacturing. The country has diverse cultural influences and a rich historical heritage.
