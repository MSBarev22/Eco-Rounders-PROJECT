Armenia faces economic challenges such as heavy dependence on limited sectors, high unemployment rates, prevalent poverty, political instability due to regional tensions, ongoing conflicts affecting stability, and corruption issues, all impacting the nation's economic growth and investment opportunities.
Armenia, located in the South Caucasus region of Eurasia, is a landlocked country bordered by Turkey, Georgia, Azerbaijan, and Iran. Yerevan serves as its capital and largest city. The population, around 3 million people, primarily speaks Armenian. Armenia operates as a democratic republic with a mixed economy reliant on industries like agriculture, mining, and technology. The country has a rich cultural heritage and historical significance, with Mount Ararat being a prominent symbol.
