Bhutan faces economic challenges such as over-reliance on hydropower exports, vulnerability to environmental shifts impacting hydropower generation, limited industry diversification, high government debt, and the necessity for sustainable economic growth while preserving its distinct cultural heritage and environment as key priorities for development and stability.
Bhutan, in South Asia, is a landlocked country bordered by China and India. Thimphu is the capital. Population: around 800,000, mainly speaking Dzongkha. It's a constitutional monarchy with an economy centered on agriculture, emerging in tourism and hydroelectric power. Known for Gross National Happiness (GNH), emphasizing sustainable development and cultural preservation.
