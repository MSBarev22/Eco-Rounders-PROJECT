Belgium's broader economic challenges encompass high public debt levels, persistent issues with sluggish economic growth attributed to factors like high taxation and bureaucratic complexities, noticeable regional disparities within the country, and external uncertainties in global trade patterns, collectively posing significant hurdles to achieving sustained economic stability and robust growth.
Belgium, situated in Western Europe, is a federal parliamentary democracy and constitutional monarchy. Brussels is the capital, hosting key EU institutions. With a population of around 11.5 million people speaking Dutch, French, and German, Belgium's economy is diverse, focused on manufacturing, services, finance, and trade, renowned for its cultural heritage and culinary specialties like chocolates, waffles, and beer.
