Greece contends with environmental challenges such as air pollution, water scarcity, and biodiversity loss. Urban areas, particularly Athens, face air quality issues due to traffic and industrial emissions. Water scarcity is a concern, exacerbated by climate change and increasing water demand. Agricultural activities and urbanization contribute to soil erosion and habitat loss, impacting biodiversity. Greece also grapples with waste management challenges, especially in coastal areas. Sustainable practices, conservation efforts, and improved waste management are essential for addressing these eco problems and enhancing Greece's environmental sustainability.
