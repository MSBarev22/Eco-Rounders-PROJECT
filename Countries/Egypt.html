Egypt grapples with a spectrum of environmental challenges that impact its ecosystems and the livelihoods of its people. Water scarcity is a pressing issue, exacerbated by the country's dependency on the Nile River and increasing water demand from a growing population. Pollution in the Nile, stemming from agricultural runoff, industrial discharges, and inadequate waste management, poses risks to both human health and aquatic ecosystems. Land degradation and desertification are prevalent due to factors such as overgrazing, deforestation, and improper agricultural practices, further exacerbated by climate change. Air pollution, primarily from vehicular emissions and industrial activities, contributes to respiratory problems in urban areas.
