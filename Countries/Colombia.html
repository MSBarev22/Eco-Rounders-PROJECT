Colombia faces various environmental challenges that impact its diverse ecosystems. Deforestation, largely driven by agriculture, logging, and illegal activities, threatens biodiversity and contributes to soil erosion. The country also contends with water pollution, stemming from agricultural runoff and inadequate waste management practices, affecting both aquatic ecosystems and human health. Illegal mining operations, particularly for gold, result in habitat destruction and chemical pollution. Additionally, Colombia is vulnerable to the impacts of climate change, experiencing phenomena like extreme weather events and shifting agricultural patterns. Striking a balance between economic development and environmental conservation is crucial as Colombia addresses these challenges and works towards sustainable practices.
