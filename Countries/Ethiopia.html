Ethiopia confronts significant environmental challenges, including deforestation, soil degradation, and water scarcity. Widespread agricultural practices, logging, and population growth contribute to extensive deforestation, disrupting ecosystems and threatening biodiversity. Soil degradation, exacerbated by unsustainable farming methods, poses a threat to agricultural productivity and food security. Water scarcity is a pressing issue, intensified by climate change, deforestation, and increased demand. Addressing these eco problems necessitates sustainable land management, reforestation initiatives, and efficient water resource management strategies in Ethiopia.
