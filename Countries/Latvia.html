Latvia faces environmental challenges such as water pollution, soil degradation, and biodiversity loss. Agriculture contributes to water pollution through runoff, impacting rivers and lakes. Soil degradation results from unsustainable land-use practices, affecting agricultural productivity. Biodiversity faces threats from habitat loss and fragmentation due to infrastructure development. Latvia also deals with air pollution, primarily in urban areas. The country is actively working on sustainable agriculture practices, conservation initiatives, and pollution control measures to address these eco problems and enhance environmental sustainability.
