Algeria faces ecological challenges such as desertification, water scarcity, soil erosion, biodiversity loss, and inadequate waste management, impacting its environment and requiring sustainable solutions.
Algeria, located in North Africa, has Algiers as its capital, with a population around 44-45 million people. Arabic is the official language, Berber is recognized, and French is commonly used. Islam is the predominant religion in this republic governed by a President and a Prime Minister.
